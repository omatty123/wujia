Key People
Kongzi (孔子, 551–479 BCE)
Founder who emphasized virtue and social harmony
Mengzi (孟子, 372–289 BCE)
Developed theory of inherently good human nature
Xunzi (荀子, 310–235 BCE)
Argued human nature requires cultivation through ritual
Laozi (老子, 6th c. BCE)
Legendary author of the Daodejing
Zhuangzi (庄子, 369–286 BCE)
Emphasized spontaneity, transformation, and freedom
Mozi (墨子, 470–391 BCE)
Advocated universal love and utilitarianism
The People (民)
Ordinary people as moral center of Mohism
Shang Yang (商鞅, 390–338 BCE)
Emphasized law (fa) and harsh punishments
Han Feizi (韩非子, 280–233 BCE)
Synthesized Legalist thought
